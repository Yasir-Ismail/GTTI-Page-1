Mechanical Technology

Precision Engineering & Manufacturing Excellence.

Department Profile

About the Department

The Mechanical Department is the backbone of industrial training at GTTI. We focus on turning raw potential into skilled craftsmanship. Our students learn to master complex machinery, from traditional lathes to modern CNC systems.

With a strong emphasis on precision and safety, we prepare students for the heavy engineering, automotive, and manufacturing sectors.

Eligibility Criteria

Education

Matriculation (Science/Arts) / Middle (for specific trades)

Age Limit

15 - 35 Years

Physical Fitness

Must be medically fit for workshop activities.

Learning Outcomes

Technical Skills

  • Operation of Lathe, Milling, and Shaper machines.
  • CNC G-Code & M-Code programming.
  • Technical Drawing reading and interpretation.

Practical Exposure

  • Manufacturing machine parts to tolerance.
  • Tool grinding and maintenance.
  • Industrial safety protocols.

Machinery Used

  • CNC Turning Centers, Vertical Machining Centers.
  • Conventional Lathes, Drills, Grinders.

Labs & Facilities

Advanced Machine Shop

A massive workshop hall housing over 40 conventional machines for individual student practice.

CNC Simulation Lab

Computer lab with simulation software to dry-run programs before machining.

Metrology Lab

Precision measurement room with Vernier Calipers, Micrometers, and Height Gauges.
